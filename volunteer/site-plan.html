Content

Home page

Opportunities to serve and volunteer are all around us. Especially when the need is constant! Serving others is how we show our love to God and to our neighbors. Remember what Jesus said in Matthew 25:40: "ye have done it unto me."

Just Me

If you want to volunteer today by yourself, click here! We'll show you the local shelters and resource centers that could use your help immediately. These locations often need volunteers to serve breakfast, lunch, and dinner, so there are many regularly recurring time slots that are sure to fit your schedule. You'll likely meet other like-minded people too!

With My Friends

You can also volunteer with your friends or youth group. You'll need to plan a bit ahead, since these places need to consider how they can accomodate large groups. For some of these, you show up with your group and the organization will provide everything. For others, you get to plan, prep, cook, serve, and clean up! Click here to find out more.

Just Me

You can sign up to volunteer immediately at these fine organizations.

St. Vincent de Paul Dining Hall

Sponsored by Catholic Community Services, this industrially designed kitchen prepares hot, fresh, and nutritious meals that are both served in their dining area to the needy and trucked to multiple shelters and resource centers in the greater Salt Lake area. You can sign up to prepare food or serve food.

  • Serve people experiencing homelessness and multiple shelters in the area
  • Prepare meals in their kitchen or serve meals
  • 437 W 200 S Salt Lake City, UT 84101
  • Volunteer

Pamela Atkinson Resource Center

This resource center for people experiencing homelessness provides hot meals, counseling services, and a safe place to sleep. It's sponsored by The Road Home, and they have a regular need for volunteers.

  • Serve people experiencing homelessness
  • Serve hot meals for breakfast, lunch, and dinner, or organize in-kind donations
  • 3380 S 1000 W, South Salt Lake, UT 84119
  • Volunteer

Recovery on Redwood

This men's detoxification center provides counseling and hot fresh meals, cooked on the premises. The goal is to assist those seeking detox and withdrawal management back on their feet.

  • Serve men utilizing the provided detox and counseling services
  • Serve lunch or dinner
  • 1875 S Redwood Rd, Salt Lake City, UT 84104
  • Volunteer

With My Friends

Volunteering with your friends or youth group is a great way to serve the community and grow closer together. The opportunities listed below all require a bit of planning in advance. Read on to find what fits your group!

Utah Food Bank

The food bank buys food and accepts donations to distribute to various organizations and directly to households. They can take a single dollar and stretch it $8 worth of food! This volunteering activity is great for youth groups and work groups.

  • Serve people experiencing food insecurity all across the State of Utah
  • Participate in various projects, such as sorting donations, and packing cases with non-perishable food to send to households
  • 3150 S 900 W, South Salt Lake, UT 84119
  • Volunteer

Youth Resource Center

The resource center for homeless youth is sponsored by the Volunteers of America. They provide counseling and mental health services for youth ages 15-22. Youth can drop in for help or stay at the shelter as needed.

  • Serve youth experiencing homelessness
  • Plan, bring, prepare, serve, and clean up after meals
  • 888 S 400 W, Salt Lake City, UT 84101
  • Volunteer
